Justin Boyden is the Founder and Head Coach of Backyard Bullies Wrestling Club, a wrestling organization dedicated to developing athletes through discipline, hard work, and community involvement.

Originally from Beckley, West Virginia, Coach Boyden served more than 20 years in the U.S. military, including multiple combat deployments. Throughout his military career, wrestling remained a constant passion and source of personal growth. As a competitive athlete, he earned recognition as a two-time All-Army Wrestler and developed a coaching philosophy centered on toughness, accountability, and resilience.

Coach Boyden is committed to creating opportunities for all wrestlers regardless of experience level. He has been instrumental in expanding girls wrestling opportunities, organizing free community practices, hosting camps and clinics, and helping athletes pursue their goals on the national stage. His dedication to athlete development has also earned him opportunities to serve as a coach with Team Georgia Wrestling's national team staff.

Beyond wins and losses, Coach Boyden believes wrestling is a vehicle for building character, confidence, and leadership.

Wrestling FAQ

Answers to common questions from parents and athletes looking for wrestling in Augusta, GA and across the CSRA.

Is Backyard Bullies good for beginner wrestlers?

Yes. Beginner wrestlers learn stance, motion, basic attacks, defense, mat safety, and how practice works. Coaches focus on confidence, discipline, and steady progress, not throwing new athletes into situations before they are ready. Your first class is free.

What age can kids start wrestling?

Backyard Bullies works with wrestlers from pre-K and elementary age on up through middle school and high school, plus adults. Younger athletes are placed by readiness, attention span, and experience level. If you are not sure where your child fits, call or text and we will help you choose the right practice.

Do you train adults?

Yes. Adults are welcome and train within our existing classes, with placement by experience level rather than age. Whether you are brand new to wrestling or getting back on the mat, call or text and we will point you to the right practice. Your first class is free.

Do you offer girls wrestling in Augusta?

Yes. Backyard Bullies offers a girls wrestling program in Augusta, GA for female athletes of all skill levels. Girls can build fundamentals, train with serious partners, and prepare for school, club, and tournament competition.

Do you train middle school and high school wrestlers?

Yes. Middle school and high school wrestlers train technique, conditioning, live wrestling, situational wrestling, and competition skills. Advanced athletes can prepare for folkstyle season, freestyle and Greco-Roman events, and higher-level tournaments.

What is the difference between beginner, intermediate, and advanced practices?

Beginner practices focus on fundamentals and confidence. Intermediate practices add more technique, chain wrestling, conditioning, and match awareness. Advanced practices are for wrestlers preparing for tougher competition, live situations, and state, regional, or national goals.

What days and times are practices?

Practices run throughout the week, with beginner, intermediate, girls, and advanced sessions scheduled Monday through Friday. Free CSRA Folkstyle Practice is Sunday from 2:00-4:00 PM. Check the weekly schedule for the current breakdown.

How much does wrestling training cost?

Monthly membership starts at $90 for two practices per week and goes up to $150 for five practices per week. Drop-in sessions are $15. See membership and pricing for the full list.

Can my child try a class before signing up?

Yes. Your first class is free so your child can meet the coaches, try practice, and see whether Backyard Bullies is a good fit. Call or text before you come in so we can point you to the right session.
