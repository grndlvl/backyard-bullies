Read full bio for Coach Justin Boyden +

Justin Boyden is the Founder and Head Coach of Backyard Bullies Wrestling Club, a wrestling organization dedicated to developing athletes through discipline, hard work, and community involvement.

Originally from Beckley, West Virginia, Coach Boyden served more than 20 years in the U.S. military, including multiple combat deployments. Throughout his military career, wrestling remained a constant passion and source of personal growth. As a competitive athlete, he earned recognition as a two-time All-Army Wrestler and developed a coaching philosophy centered on toughness, accountability, and resilience.

After relocating to the Augusta, Georgia area, Coach Boyden founded Backyard Bullies Wrestling Club with the mission of building the wrestling community throughout the CSRA. Under his leadership, the club has grown into a respected training program serving youth, middle school, high school, and adult wrestlers. His athletes compete in Folkstyle, Freestyle, and Greco-Roman wrestling at local, state, regional, and national levels.

Coach Boyden is committed to creating opportunities for all wrestlers regardless of experience level. He has been instrumental in expanding girls wrestling opportunities, organizing free community practices, hosting camps and clinics, and helping athletes pursue their goals on the national stage. His dedication to athlete development has also earned him opportunities to serve as a coach with Team Georgia Wrestling's national team staff.

Beyond wins and losses, Coach Boyden believes wrestling is a vehicle for building character, confidence, and leadership.

Today, Coach Boyden continues to lead Backyard Bullies Wrestling Club while mentoring the next generation of wrestlers and helping grow the sport throughout the CSRA. Through his relentless commitment to service, coaching, and community, he has become a respected advocate for wrestling in Georgia and beyond.

Read full bio for Coach Dale Schmacht +

Coach Dale Schmacht was born in Rock Island, Illinois, and graduated from Cambridge High School in Cambridge, Illinois. As a high school wrestler, he was the 1978 Illinois Class A state runner-up at 138 pounds and the 1979 Illinois Class A state champion at 145 pounds. He also earned honorable mention USA Wrestling All-American recognition in 1979 and was inducted into his high school Hall of Fame in 1985.

Coach Schmacht continued his wrestling career at Augustana College, where he became a three-time NCAA Division III All-American, placing fourth in 1981 and third in both 1982 and 1983. Augustana College inducted him into its Hall of Fame in 2006.

After college, Coach Schmacht went to graduate school and earned both a PhD in Physiology and a Medical Degree. Following his General Surgery residency, he moved to Augusta and became an Assistant Professor of Trauma, Burn, and Critical Care Surgery. He later completed a two-year vascular fellowship and continued his career in academic medicine and surgery.

Coach Schmacht has remained active in local coaching for many years. At Augusta Preparatory Day School, he served for several years as an assistant football coach and track coach, and was elected into the Augusta Prep Hall of Fame as a track coach in 2025. He also spent a couple of years as their head wrestling coach.

For seven years, Coach Schmacht led a demanding, year-round Sunday practice that helped several CSRA state champions and state place winners reach their goals. He has served as the Head Wrestling Coach at Seigler's Karate Center since 2008 and has been an Assistant Coach at Backyard Bullies Wrestling Club since 2024.

Hard work, self-discipline, and achieving established goals have shaped Coach Schmacht's path in life, and he teaches with that same mindset. His goal is to help every wrestler prepare to compete at the highest level they can reach.
