Experience · Character · Commitment

Every practice · Every athlete

Athlete Expectations

  • Arrive ready, listen closely, and give honest effort.
  • Respect coaches, teammates, opponents, and the room.
  • Be coachable—mistakes are part of getting better.
  • Choose discipline, accountability, and good sportsmanship.
  • Leave the mat and your teammates better than you found them.

Bring your best to the room.

Effort is always in your control

Your support shapes how your athlete experiences the sport.

How families strengthen the team

Parent Expectations

  • Encourage effort, learning, and resilience—not only wins.
  • Let coaches coach and athletes compete.
  • Model respect for officials, opponents, coaches, and families.
  • Communicate questions calmly and at the right time.
  • Help athletes arrive prepared, on time, and ready to learn.
